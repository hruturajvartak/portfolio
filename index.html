Projects

Mini Excavator Frame – Zero Tail-Swing Design

  • Goal: Create robust, lightweight, highly maneuverable 3.5mT mini-excavator frame meeting safety & operational standards.
  • Key Contributions: Market research, competitor benchmarking, CAD modeling in Creo, material optimization, seamless hydraulics integration.
  • Analysis & Validation: FEA on critical load cases (static, lateral, impact), iterative weight optimization.
  • Impact: Improved maneuverability in confined spaces (zero tail-swing), competitive & user-friendly design.

Reduced Tail-Swing Excavator Frame – Design Process

  • Project Goal: Design 15.5mT reduced tail-swing excavator frame for confined spaces; maintain strength, stability, manufacturability.
  • Process: Market analysis, customer feedback, efficient 3D models in Creo, high-strength steel selection, full system integration.
  • FEA & Results: Static / modal analysis, iterative optimization, lighter & more maneuverable frame, improved operator comfort.

Excavator Arm Design & Structural Optimization

  • Objective: Design excavator arm with optimal pin geometry, strength, and reach.
  • Approach: Boom/bucket/pin geometry, cross-section modeling, Q345 steel, FEA-driven geometry refinements.
  • Results: Achieved required reach and reduced weight while maintaining structural integrity.

Ergonomic And Visibility Study Of Excavator Cabin

  • Objective: Improve operator comfort, visibility, safety per ISO 5006.
  • Approach: Ergonomic assessment (Creo Manikin), blind spot/masking analysis, feedback from operators.
  • Results: +15% user feedback, –10% operator fatigue, improved satisfaction & compliance.

Topology Optimization Of Excavator Frame

  • Objective: Reduce frame weight, maintain durability/safety.
  • Approach: Topology optimization (ANSYS), static/fatigue simulations, reinforced production geometry.
  • Results: 15% lighter frame, $1.2M annual savings, improved fuel efficiency, structural reliability.

IoT-enabled Digital Twin For Structural Systems

  • Objective: Ensure durability/reliability under dynamic loads.
  • Approach: Sensor networks, IoT gateways, cloud data, digital twin modeling, real-time monitoring.
  • Results: 92% fatigue prediction, 30% downtime reduction, 20% maintenance savings.

Additive Manufacturing Of Hydraulic Manifolds

  • Objective: Reduce lead time & cost for hydraulic manifold prototyping/production.
  • Approach: SolidWorks design, ANSYS topology optimization, DMLS process, pressure/fatigue testing.
  • Results: Lead time reduced: 6 weeks → 2 weeks; 18% less material use; $500K annual savings.
